Tesla

I work on new programs at Tesla — from high-level planning and cross-team integration, to detailed part design, reliability spec generation and validation, and ramping at hyper scale. I'm the design owner of the high voltage charge port on Cybercab, an intricate system with electrical, mechanical, and thermal requirements, integrated sensors and an actuator, built using new manufacturing techniques.

The system was handed to me in the prototype phase. I led a small team through a complete design overhaul to prepare it for hyper-scale production, with a focus on reliability and manufacturing simplicity — working closely with both the upstream supplier and the downstream manufacturing team. The design shipped into full production with very low failure rates.

FSAE Formula Electric

I was a founding member of the Formula SAE Electric team at BYU. Starting from scratch with no car, no budget, and no playbook, we built a fully electric formula-style racecar and sent it to competition within two years.

Turntable

I'm building a Bluetooth device that lives on my desk and gives physical controls for music and meetings — inspired by the Teenage Engineering OB-4. The centerpiece is a turntable-style platter that spins as audio plays and lets you scrub back through a song or a meeting recording.

Under the hood: an ESP32 dual-core chip (one core for motor control, one for audio and Bluetooth), a brushless DC gimbal motor driven by SimpleFOC with careful cogging compensation, and a ring buffer that stores the last two minutes of audio. The whole thing runs off USB-C at under 0.1 W in normal operation. This project is in progress.
